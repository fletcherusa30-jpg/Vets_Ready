5. Claims Readiness Module

Understanding VA Claims

What is a VA Disability Claim? A request to the Department of Veterans Affairs for compensation due to disabilities incurred or aggravated during military service. Three Elements Required: 1. Current Diagnosed Condition - Medical evidence 2. In-Service Event - Service records, buddy statements 3. Nexus - Medical opinion linking #1 and #2

Claim Readiness Checklist

Interactive checklist covering: ‚úÖ Gather Service Records - DD-214 (all periods of service) - Service treatment records (STRs) - Personnel records ‚úÖ Medical Evidence - Current diagnosis from provider - Treatment records - Diagnostic test results - Medication lists ‚úÖ Supporting Evidence - Buddy statements - Lay statements - Nexus letters - Independent medical opinions (IMO) ‚úÖ Service Connection Theory - Direct service connection - Secondary conditions - Aggravation - Presumptive conditions ‚úÖ Timeline Documentation - Date of diagnosis - Date symptoms began - Continuity of symptoms - Treatment history

Rating Education

Understanding Disability Ratings: - Ratings from 0% to 100% in 10% increments - Based on 38 CFR Part 4 diagnostic codes - Severity determines percentage - Multiple conditions combine using "VA Math" Common Rating Levels: - 10%: Mild symptoms, occasional flare-ups - 30%: Moderate symptoms, regular impact - 50%: Severe symptoms, occupational impairment - 70%: Very severe, major life impact - 100%: Total occupational and social impairment Special Ratings: - TDIU: Total Disability Individual Unemployability (scheduler at 70%+ but can't work) - P&T: Permanent and Total (100% not expected to improve) - SMC: Additional compensation for severe disabilities

Evidence Mapping

What Evidence Supports Service Connection? 1. Medical Evidence (50% Weight) - Diagnosis from licensed provider - Medical records showing current condition - Treatment notes - Test results (MRI, X-ray, lab work) 2. Service Evidence (25% Weight) - Service medical records - Incident reports - Performance evaluations - Awards and decorations 3. Lay Evidence (15% Weight) - Your statement describing onset - Buddy statements from fellow service members - Spousal observations - Employment impact 4. Nexus Evidence (10% Weight) - Medical opinion letter - IMO (Independent Medical Opinion) - IME (Independent Medical Examination) - DBQ (Disability Benefits Questionnaire)

C&P Exam Preparation

What is a C&P Exam? Compensation & Pension examination conducted by VA or contractor to evaluate your claimed disabilities. Preparation Steps: ‚úÖ Before the Exam - Review your claim - Bring ALL medical records - Arrive 15 minutes early - Dress comfortably - Bring list of medications ‚úÖ During the Exam - Be honest and thorough - Describe worst days, not best days - Mention all symptoms - Take your time - Ask for breaks if needed ‚úÖ After the Exam - Review exam report (request copy) - Identify any errors or omissions - Submit corrections if needed - Add additional evidence if necessary Common Mistakes to Avoid: - ‚ùå Downplaying symptoms - ‚ùå Not mentioning secondary conditions - ‚ùå Saying "I'm fine" when you're not - ‚ùå Rushing through questions - ‚ùå Being vague about frequency/severity
