
Air Force Transition Readiness Guide - Full Replacement Edition
Introduction
Provide a detailed overview of the transition process specifically for U.S. Air Force Airmen. Discuss emotional, financial, professional, and logistical challenges unique to Airmen. Include notes on Air Force culture, high-tech career fields, deployment cycles, and the shift from structured military life to civilian autonomy.

Provide detailed tasks, checklists, and explanations for Airmen beginning transition 12 months out. Include Airman & Family Readiness Center (A&FRC) TAP enrollment, medical readiness, dental readiness, record retrieval, SkillBridge eligibility, AFSC-based credentialing pathways, and family readiness.
9-Month Timeline (Air Force-Specific)
Include AFSC translation guidance, credentialing opportunities through Air Force COOL, VA health enrollment, networking, mentorship programs (e.g., ACP), relocation research, and childcare planning.
6-Month Timeline (Air Force-Specific)
Include resume building, job search strategy, VA disability claim preparation, housing planning, interview preparation, financial forecasting, and A&FRC resources.
3-Month Timeline (Air Force-Specific)
Include final medical appointments, legal readiness, relocation planning, family support coordination, final TAP workshops, and job application surge.
Final Out-Processing (Air Force-Specific)
Include clearing procedures, unit checkout, medical/dental finalization, DD-214 verification, TRICARE transition, and household goods coordination.
12 Months Post-Separation (Air Force-Specific)
Include VA Solid Start calls, community integration, employment stabilization, continuing education, and long-term financial planning.
